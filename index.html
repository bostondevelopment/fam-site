What Fam is

Fam is a private family graph. The people you love, the photos you've taken of them, the birthdays and anniversaries and holidays that mark a year together — all in one place, visible only to your family.

It's not a social network and it's not a chat app. There are no followers, no public posts, no algorithm picking who sees what. Just your family, your memories, and a quiet running feed of what's coming up next.

How to use it

Four steps from empty app to a family that's actually together.

  1. Start a family. Name it. Invite the people you trust.
  2. Add your people. Parents, grandparents, kids, in-laws, the aunt nobody talks about. Birthdays and anniversaries go on the calendar automatically.
  3. Tag the photos. Drop in old albums. Fam suggests who's in each face. You confirm or correct.
  4. Open the Pulse. Whose birthday is next, what just got added, who needs a card in the mail.

The habits that make it feel alive

Fam quietly accumulates the more you give it. A few minutes a week is plenty.

  • Add a birthday the day you think of it — never lose another one.
  • Tag a face or two when you scroll your camera roll. Old albums fill themselves in.
  • RSVP to family events instead of ghosting the group text.
  • Drop a memory on someone's profile when they cross your mind.

A month in, this is the screen you open when you want to feel close to your family.

The Pulse

The home of Fam is the Pulse — a quiet running feed of what's happening in your family. Birthdays coming up. Photos just added. Memories someone wrote down. RSVPs you owe. Pulled together in one place, scoped to your family. No algorithm picking what you see.
